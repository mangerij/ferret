InterfaceDiffusiveFluxIntegral

Computes the diffusive flux on the interface.

The diffusive flux integral is defined as with the interface, the diffusion coefficient, the variable and the normal to the interface.

The integral of the diffusive flux may be used to compute the contribution of diffusion when examining the balance of a physical quantity (energy, momentum) over a domain.

commentnote

For finite volume variables, this postprocessor computes the diffusive flux using a two point gradient. This is only accurate if the (interface) kernel is also computing gradients this way. Currently, only FVDiffusionInterface is computing gradients this way.

warningwarning

The expression of the diffusive flux in this object is generic, as described, and may differ from the diffusive flux in your specific physics implementation. If so, you may not use this object to compute the diffusive flux.
